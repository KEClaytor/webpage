Nonlinear Oscillator

One of the coolest things that I like about physics is how accessible it can be with some fairly simple experiments. Here I've built a pendulum out of Legos, it has a rubber band to provide a damping force, and a motor to drive it. Despite its relative simplicity, we can observe some fairly complicated behavior, such as bifurcation (different behavior for the same conditions).

As a project for a nonlinear dynamics class I took, I constructed this driven, damped oscillator. It exhibits hysteresis in the resonance frequency. For example, if you increase the frequency that you're driving it at, you won't always get the same amplitude of oscillation as if you had reached the same frequency by decreasing the drive frequency from something higher.
